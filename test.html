ANIMAL KINGDOM

ANIMAL GROUPS

Animals are divided into seven groups, these are:
Mammals
Reptiles
Amphibians
Fish
Arthropods
Mollusks
Birds

MAMMALS

Mammals are animals that have hair, are warm-blooded and drink milk when they are babies. Examples are
cats
dogs
Other examples includes rats, mice, whales, dolphins,lions, tigers, wolves, humans, etc

REPTILES

Reptiles have scales, breathe air, are cold blooded and usually lay eggs on land. Examples are
snake
turtle
Other example includes alligators, iguanas, lizards,etc

AMPHIBIANS

Amphibians spend part of their lives in water and part on land and they are cold blooded. Examples are
newts
toads
Other examples includes frogs, salamander etc

FISH

Fish live in water, have gills to breathe, scales and fins on their bodies, and are cold-blooded

ARTHROPODS

Arthropods are the most numerous animals on earth. They have an exoskeleton, a segmented body, and jointed legs. They do not have backbones and are cold-blooded. Some efamples are
bees
butterfly
Other exambles includes crab, scorpion, ladybird, millipedes, spiders, etc

MOLLUSKS

Mollusks are soft-bodied animals and many have hard external shells. Some examples are
snails
octopus
Other examples are squids,clams, oysters, scallops etc

BIRDS

Birds are warm-blooded, have wings, feathers, a beak and their babies hatch from eggs.
